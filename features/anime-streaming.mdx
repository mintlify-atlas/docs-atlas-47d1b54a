## Home feed

When you open Anikin, the home screen displays anime grouped into categories sourced from AniList. Use the category selector bar at the top to switch between:

| Category | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Popular** | All-time most popular anime, sorted by popularity |
| **Recently Updated** | Episodes that aired in the last 7 days |
| **Current Season** | Anime airing in the current seasonal window (Winter / Spring / Summer / Fall) |
| **Trending** | Titles trending right now by popularity |
| **New Season** | Anime from the upcoming/current Winter season |
| **Feminine Audience** | Shoujo-tagged titles sorted by popularity |
| **Male Audience** | Shounen-tagged titles sorted by popularity |
| **Trash Anime** | Titles with the lowest favourite counts on AniList, sorted by favourites ascending |

All categories exclude adult content and, by default, only show Japanese-origin titles. You can enable non-Japanese anime under **Settings → App Settings**.

## Opening a show

Tap any cover art in the home feed to open the show's detail page. The page shows the cover image, title, genre tags, and a star rating pulled from AniList. Two tabs are available at the bottom of the cover area:

- **Overview** — synopsis and metadata
- **Episodes** — the full episode list for the selected provider

## Sub / dub toggle

At the top of the **Episodes** tab you'll find a **Sub / Dub** toggle. Switching it reloads the episode list from the current provider using the dub version of the title (appending `(dub)` to the search query). Not all providers carry dub tracks; if no results are found, the app falls back to a manual provider search sheet.

## Episode list

Episodes are displayed in a scrollable grid. Several options let you customise the layout:

- **Sort order** — toggle between ascending (episode 1 first) and descending (latest first). The setting is saved between sessions.
- **Episode ranges** — for shows with more than 50 episodes, the list is split into chunks of 50. Range buttons (e.g., `1 - 50`, `51 - 100`) appear above the grid so you can jump to any segment.
- **Grid layout** — switch between **Semi** (compact cards) and **Full** (larger cards with more detail) using the layout icon in the header. Your preference is saved automatically.

Episodes that you have already started watching display a progress bar showing how far through the episode you got.

## Changing the source provider

Tap the **server icon** (DNS icon) in the top-right corner of the show page to open the provider picker. On Android a bottom sheet slides up; on Windows an action sheet appears. Select any provider from the list and the episode list reloads from that source. The selection is remembered as your default provider for future visits.

If the app cannot automatically match the show title against the selected provider, a **provider search sheet** opens so you can search and select the correct entry manually.

## How to watch an episode

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a show">
    Tap a title from the home feed or search results to open the show's detail page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the Episodes tab">
    Tap the **Episodes** tab. The app automatically searches the active provider for the best title match using the Romaji, Native, and English titles in that order.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select an episode">
    Tap an episode card to go directly to the video player, which loads the default server and quality automatically.

    Alternatively, long-press an episode card to open the **source sheet**, where you can choose a specific server or quality before playback begins.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Watch">
    The video player starts streaming. Use the on-screen controls to pause, seek, change playback speed, or skip to the next or previous episode.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Video player

The player launches in landscape mode by default. Controls available during playback:

- **Previous / Next episode** buttons to move between episodes without leaving the player
- **Playback speed selector** — standard speeds from 0.25× to 2× (or "cursed" speeds up to 50× if enabled in player settings)
- **Subtitle overlay** — subtitles in your system language are loaded automatically when available from the source (VTT and SRT formats are supported)
- **Source sheet** — if the current video fails, the app shows a "Try another source" prompt; you can also open the source sheet manually to switch servers mid-playback

<Note>
  On Android, if the video fails to load, a toast message appears. On Windows, an alert dialog is shown instead.
</Note>

## Episode progress tracking

Progress is saved locally on your device every time you pause, stop, or leave the player.

Each episode is stored under the key `{mediaId}-{episodeNumber}` in a local file (`PlayerSettings.dat`). The saved data includes:

- **WatchedDuration** — position in milliseconds, used to resume from where you left off
- **WatchedPercentage** — percentage watched, shown as a progress bar on the episode card

When you return to the episode list, progress indicators are refreshed automatically so you can see at a glance which episodes you have started or completed.

<Tip>
  If you have an AniList account connected, you can also sync watch progress to your AniList profile. See [Favourites and watchlist](/features/favourites-and-watchlist) for details.
</Tip>
